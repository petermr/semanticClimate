interglacial or interglaciation

A globally warm period lasting thousands of years between glacial periods within an ice age.

Generally coincides with odd-numbered marine isotope stages (MIS) when mean sea level was close to present. The Last Interglacial (LIG) occurred between about 129 and 116 ka (thousand years) before present (defined as 1950) although the warm period started in some areas a few thousand years earlier. In terms of MIS, interglaciations are defined as the interval between the midpoint of the preceding termination and the onset of the next glaciation.
