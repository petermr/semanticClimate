'medlineAbbreviation': 'Sci Rep', 'NLMid': '101563288', 'ISSN': '2045-2322', 'ESSN': '2045-2322'}} 2024 12464 The change detection (CD) technology has greatly improved the ability to interpret land surface changes. Deep learning (DL) methods have been widely used in the field of CD due to its high detection accuracy and application range. DL-based CD methods usually cannot fuse the extracted feature information at full scale, leaving out effective information, and commonly use transfer learning methods, which rely on the original dataset and training weights. To address the above issues, we propose a deeply supervised (DS) change detection network (DASUNet) that fuses full-scale features, which adopts a Siamese architecture, fuses full-scale feature information, and realizes end-to-end training. In order to obtain higher feature information, the network uses atrous spatial pyramid pooling (ASPP) module in the coding stage. In addition, the DS module is used in the decoding stage to exploit feature information at each scale in the final prediction. The experimental comparison shows that the proposed network has the current state-of-the-art performance on the CDD and the WHU-CD, reaching 94.32% and 90.37% on F1, respectively.
